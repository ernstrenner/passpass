How it works

This tool applies three simple transformations to your input:

  1. ROT-N cipher: shifts letters forward by your chosen number
  2. Spaces are removed
  3. The result is reversed

PassPass runs fully in your browser. It stores nothing — and works offline.

For real-life use cases and printable instructions, see the README or the blog post.

What is ROT?

ROT stands for "rotate." A ROT-7 cipher means each letter is shifted forward 7 places in the alphabet.

For example: A → H, B → I, ..., Z → G. It loops around after Z.

Only alphabet letters are changed. Numbers, punctuation, and emojis stay the same.
